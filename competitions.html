COMPETITIONS

Astralis: The Future Of Space Robotics

CASI TMU Branch 2025

As part of a three-member team, I competed in the Astralis: Future of Robotics competition, developing a mitigation strategy for space debris accumulation in LEO and GEO orbits.

Our concept deployed a network of orbit-based sensor modules to create a real-time data-sharing infrastructure. We pitched our solution to judges from MDA Space, Rocket Lab, and NordSpace.

CATIA C3 Aerospace Design

1st Place - Senior Category

We secured 1st Place for our innovative long-endurance glider design, Galforce. The challenge required conceptualizing a propulsion-free, 3-meter wingspan glider using CATIA V5.

I led the design of the fuselage and modular payload bay, utilizing carbon-fiber-reinforced composites to minimize weight while maintaining structural integrity.

AER507 Glider Competition

Design, Manufacturing & Flight

Read Full Report

The goal of this project was to design and construct a glider aircraft capable of flying the furthest possible distance while weighing the least. We utilized the NACA M22 airfoil and manufactured the wing using composite materials to optimize lift and durability.

Several requirements had to be met: the final design length had to be under 29.5 inches, fuselage width under 2 inches, and tail fin area less than 30% of the wing area. All non-wing components were designed in CAD and manufactured using laser cutting and 3D printing to optimize weight and durability.

The competition scored designs based on glide distance divided by final weight. Our final glider successfully survived all three flight attempts, demonstrating the effectiveness of our manufacturing process. The final design balanced structural integrity with aerodynamic efficiency.
