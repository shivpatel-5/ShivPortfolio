Airbus A380 CAD Modeling

SolidWorks Surface Design & Assembly

To kickstart the Winter 2026 term, I challenged myself to model the massive Airbus A380 in SolidWorks within a single week. This project demanded a deep application of advanced assembly and 3D sketching, followed by complex lofting techniques to capture the iconic aerodynamic profile of the superjumbo jet.

I utilized surface modeling to generate the complex curvature of the wings and vertical stabilizer, ensuring smooth continuity across the airframe.

Walking Robot

Mechatronics & Kinematics

This report showcases the design, development, and analysis of a 4-legged walking robot that utilizes a four-bar linkage mechanism (Crank - Rocker Mechanism). The robot is driven by a motor with a gear ratio of 126. The objective was to manufacture a robot capable of walking efficiently while showcasing mechanical stability.

The research phase explored three distinct mechanical designs:

  • A Double Crank-Rocker Mechanism
  • A Crank-slider Mechanism
  • A Crank-Rocker Mechanism

Among all the configurations, The Double Crank-Rocker Mechanism showcased excellence in speed, simplicity, and minimal material usage, scoring the highest in the comparative design matrix.

The selected design features a six-bar linkage comprised of two synchronized four-bar linkages, phased 180 degrees from each other. The links were modeled using CATIA V5 and laser cut onto an 8mm MDF sheet.
