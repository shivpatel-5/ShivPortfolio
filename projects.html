ENGINEERING PROJECTS

Airbus A380 CAD Modeling

SolidWorks Surface Design & Assembly

To kickstart the Winter 2026 term, I challenged myself to model the massive Airbus A380 in SolidWorks within a single week. This project demanded a deep application of advanced assembly and 3D sketching, followed by complex lofting techniques to capture the iconic aerodynamic profile of the superjumbo jet.

I utilized surface modeling to generate the complex curvature of the wings and vertical stabilizer, ensuring smooth continuity across the airframe. While this current iteration focuses on the primary structural geometry, I am actively developing the next phase, which will involve integrating the high-bypass turbofan engines and detailed landing gear assemblies to complete the digital twin.

DA50 RG Aircraft Design

CATIA V5 Surface Modeling

This project involved the detailed surface modeling of a DA50 RG aircraft using CATIA V5. The focus was on aerodynamic accuracy and mastering the Generative Shape Design workbench.

Key challenges included creating smooth transitions between the fuselage and the wings, as well as modeling the intake manifolds to match real-world specifications. This model serves as a base for future CFD analysis.

Composite Wing Structure

Materials & Manufacturing

A hands-on engineering project focused on the internal structural integrity of an airfoil. We designed and assembled a wing skeleton to support composite skin application.

This project highlighted the importance of Rib and Spar placement for load distribution. We utilized balsa and plywood for the frame, ensuring it remained lightweight while providing the necessary rigidity for the carbon fiber skinning process.
